# **Dashboard Visualizations & Chart Descriptions**

### **1. Revenue Trends (2017 - 2019)**
Chart Type: Area/Line Chart

Description: This chart tracks the total revenue generated over three years.

Data Insight: There is a noticeable downward trend, starting at $3.44M in 2017 and decreasing to $2.92M in 2019, signaling a need for refreshed marketing strategies or product updates.

### **2. Geographic Revenue Distribution**
Chart Type: Bar Chart

Description: A breakdown of revenue across the seven states.

Data Insight: South Carolina ($1.43M) and Florida ($1.41M) are the leading contributors to total revenue, closely followed by Georgia.

### **3. Customer Acquisition Channels**
Chart Type: Donut Chart

Description: Visualizes the sources of customer traffic: Ads (1,982), Organic (1,947), and Returning Customers (1,851).

Data Insight: The split is remarkably balanced, though paid advertising currently leads as the primary driver of new customers.

### **4. Delivery Performance**
Chart Type: Gauge / Pie Chart

Description: Measures the percentage of "On-Time" vs. "Delayed" deliveries.

Data Insight: 67.28% of deliveries are on-time, while 32.71% are delayed. This high delay rate is a critical operational bottleneck that impacts satisfaction.

### **5. Product Return Analysis**
Chart Type: Pie Chart

Description: Displays the ratio of products returned by customers.

Data Insight: Only 10.31% of orders resulted in returns, indicating high product quality and accurate customer expectations.

### **6. Customer Satisfaction Heatmap (Product vs. Rating)**
Chart Type: Stacked Bar Chart

Description: Breaks down satisfaction levels (1 - Very Low to 5 - Very High) across five different product lines.

Data Insight: Most products fall into the "(3) OK" category (2,308 counts). Product 1 and Product 2 maintain the highest volume of positive feedback.

### **Key Insights & Recommendations**

**Based on the Performance Analysis Report:**

Retention: Customer retention remains strong at roughly 32% of total acquisitions, suggesting that loyalty programs are effective.

Logistics: The 32% delivery delay rate is identified as the primary risk to future sales and customer ratings.

Growth: While revenue is currently declining, there is significant opportunity to scale in top-performing regions like South Carolina and Georgia through targeted regional campaigns.

### **Recommendations:**
Logistics Overhaul: Review the transportation system to reduce delivery delays below 15%.

Satisfaction Incentives: Launch follow-up surveys for "OK" rated customers and reward "Very High" satisfaction users with loyalty discounts.

Market Strategy: Focus marketing spend on "Returning" customer segments to improve Lifetime Value (LTV).
